PokeFlex: Towards a Real-World Dataset of Deformable Objects in Robotic Manipulation

Jan Obrist1, Miguel Zamora1, Hehui Zheng1,2, Juan Zarate3, Stelian Coros1,
1Computational Robotics Lab, Department of Computer Science, ETH Zurich
2Soft Robotics Lab, Department of Mechanical Engineering, ETH Zurich
3Advanced Interactive Technologies Lab, Department of Computer Science, ETH Zurich

PokeFlex is a dataset containing 3D mesh models of deformable objects.

Abstract

Manipulating deformable objects is a significant challenge in robotics due to their high-dimensional and complex physical properties. While data-driven methods have shown potential for solving manipulation tasks, their application in the domain of deformable objects has been constrained by the lack of data capturing the deformation behaviour of objects. To address this, we introduce the PokeFlex dataset, featuring real-world 3D mesh data of deformed objects, along with the applied forces by a robotic arm poking the objects. Deformations are captured with a professional volumetric capture system that allows for complete 360-degree reconstruction. The PokeFlex dataset consists of five deformable objects with varying stiffness and shapes. Additionally, using the PokeFlex dataset, we train a model capable of predicting deformations in real-time with a single image and the template mesh. We refer readers to the supplementary material for demos and examples of our dataset.
